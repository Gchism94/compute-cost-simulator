# Discussion Prompts

## Opening Prompts

- When have you treated computation as “free” because you were not directly paying for it?
- What kinds of costs are hidden in classroom computing environments?
- How might limited budgets change what students choose to try?

## After The Flagship Demo

- Which step should the student have estimated earlier?
- Was the expensive model justified by its F1 gain?
- Should the student choose the best model, the cheapest model, or a middle option?
- What would you do differently if the workflow had to be reproducible by every student?

## Token Economics

- What makes a prompt expensive?
- When is extra context helpful, and when is it wasteful?
- How should students decide whether to paste a whole file, a traceback, or a smaller excerpt?

## RAG And AI Workflows

- Why is RAG not free just because the documents are “ours”?
- Which RAG layer is easiest to overlook: embedding, indexing, retrieval, context, or generation?
- How can chat history quietly increase cost?
- What habits make AI-assisted coding more cost-aware?

## Scaling

- Why does a cost that looks tiny per action matter at classroom scale?
- What changes when a workflow runs weekly instead of once?
- What should be estimated before deployment?

## Closing Prompts

- What would you put in a cost-aware project report?
- What is one action you would skip, reduce, or redesign after seeing its cost?
- How can cost-aware computing support fairness and reproducibility?
