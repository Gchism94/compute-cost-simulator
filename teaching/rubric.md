# Rubric: Cost-Aware Computing Assignment

Total: 100 points

## Cost Tracking: 15 Points

- 15: Uses CCS consistently for all major actions and includes clear receipts.
- 12: Tracks most major actions, with minor omissions.
- 8: Tracks some actions but leaves important costs out.
- 5: Mentions cost but does not use receipts clearly.
- 0: Does not address cost.

## Pre-Run Estimation And Decision-Making: 15 Points

- 15: Estimates expensive actions before running and uses estimates to decide what to run.
- 12: Includes estimates but decision-making is only partly explained.
- 10: Estimates are present but not connected to choices.
- 5: Estimates appear after execution only.
- 0: No pre-run estimation.

## Cost-Performance Analysis: 15 Points

- 15: Compares performance, cost, cost per metric, marginal gain, and marginal cost.
- 12: Compares performance and cost with limited marginal analysis.
- 10: Reports cost and performance separately.
- 5: Reports only one side of the tradeoff.
- 0: No meaningful comparison.

## Reasoning And Justification: 20 Points

- 20: Explains choices clearly using evidence and independent judgment.
- 15: Reasoning is mostly clear but misses some evidence or alternatives.
- 10: Reasoning is present but relies too much on tool output or unsupported claims.
- 5: Reasoning is vague or mostly descriptive.
- 0: No clear reasoning.

## Final Recommendation: 15 Points

- 15: Recommends a workflow with a clear, evidence-based tradeoff argument.
- 12: Recommendation is reasonable but missing some evidence.
- 10: Recommendation is stated but weakly justified.
- 5: Recommendation is unclear.
- 0: No recommendation.

## Reflection On Reproducibility And Scale: 10 Points

- 10: Explains whether the workflow is reproducible and projects cost at larger scale.
- 8: Addresses reproducibility or scale well, but not both.
- 5: Mentions reproducibility or scale briefly.
- 2: Reflection is vague.
- 0: No reflection.

## AI Transparency And Responsible Use: 10 Points

- 10: Clearly reports AI use, tracks AI-related cost, and explains student decisions.
- 8: Reports AI use and cost but needs more detail about decisions.
- 5: Mentions AI use but does not explain cost or role clearly.
- 2: AI use is unclear or mostly replaces student thinking.
- 0: Does not disclose AI use when AI appears to have been used.

## Independent Thinking Note

High scores require your own reasoning. Using AI tools is allowed when your
instructor permits it, but tool usage is not a substitute for explaining your
choices, checking outputs, and making the final decision yourself.
