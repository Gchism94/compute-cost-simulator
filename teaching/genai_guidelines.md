# Generative AI Guidelines

You may use generative AI tools in this assignment, but your use must be
transparent, cost-aware, and focused on learning.

## Allowed Uses

- Brainstorming approaches.
- Asking for explanations of concepts or errors.
- Getting feedback on code you wrote.
- Drafting tests, comments, or documentation.
- Summarizing results after you understand them.

## Boundaries

- Do not submit AI output you do not understand.
- Do not let an AI tool make your final recommendation for you.
- Do not hide AI usage.
- Do not paste private, sensitive, or prohibited data into an AI tool.
- Follow any additional course, institution, or project rules from your instructor.

## Transparency Requirement

Include an AI Usage Statement with your submission. State:

- Which AI tools you used.
- What you used them for.
- How many AI calls or major chat turns you made, if known.
- What you changed after reviewing the AI output.
- Which decisions were your own.

## Cost Awareness

AI usage contributes to simulated cost. Long prompts, pasted files, tracebacks,
chat history, retrieved context, and repeated vague questions can all increase
token use.

Before using AI support, ask:

1. Can I ask a smaller, more specific question?
2. Can I test locally before asking again?
3. Do I need to paste the full file, or only the relevant lines?
4. Is the expected benefit worth the token cost?

## Learning Goal

The goal is not to avoid AI. The goal is to use AI deliberately, explain what it
helped with, and make your own cost-performance decision.
