1. Title:
Cost-Aware Model Selection Challenge

2. Overview:
Students will compare computational approaches under a simulated compute budget and justify their final choice using performance, cost, scalability, and reproducibility.

3. Learning Goals:
- Explain that compute has cost.
- Compare performance and cost.
- Identify when expensive models are not justified.
- Estimate classroom/project-scale cost.
- Reflect on AI tool usage.

4. Setup:
- pip install -e .
- run examples/flagship_budget_failure_demo.py
- optionally run examples/realistic_usage_scenarios_demo.py

5. Student Task:
- Use a fixed simulated budget.
- Run or simulate at least three approaches.
- Include at least one cheap baseline.
- Include at least one more expensive approach.
- Track all actions with CCS.

6. GenAI Usage Statement:
Require students to report:
- tools used
- approximate number of interactions
- what they used AI for
- one suggestion they accepted
- one suggestion they rejected

7. Manual Requirement:
At least one model or workflow decision must be made without AI-generated code.

8. Reflection Questions:
Use direct student-facing language.

9. Submission Checklist:
- receipt log
- short written justification
- budget summary
- final model choice
- AI usage statement

10. Rubric:
Include clear point categories for:
- cost tracking
- model comparison
- budget reasoning
- scalability/reproducibility
- AI transparency
- clarity
